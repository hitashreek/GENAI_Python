Bhakt Prahlad Story

Bhakt Prahlad was a great devotee of Lord Vishnu and the son of the
demon king Hiranyakashipu.

Hiranyakashipu was a powerful king who wanted everyone to worship only
him. He had received a special boon that made him nearly impossible to
kill. Because of his power, he became arrogant and declared himself as
God.

However, his own son Prahlad was a devoted follower of Lord Vishnu. From
a very young age, Prahlad would chant Vishnu’s name and refused to
worship his father.

This made Hiranyakashipu very angry. He tried many times to kill
Prahlad, but each time Prahlad was saved by Lord Vishnu.

Attempts to kill Prahlad: - He was thrown from a mountain, but he
survived. - He was trampled by elephants, but remained unharmed. - He
was poisoned, but nothing happened to him. - He was thrown into fire
with his aunt Holika, who had a boon to not burn in fire. However,
Holika was burned, and Prahlad was saved by divine grace.

Finally, Hiranyakashipu angrily asked Prahlad, “Where is your God?”

Prahlad replied, “God is everywhere.”

In rage, Hiranyakashipu hit a pillar and asked if Vishnu was inside it.
Suddenly, from the pillar appeared Narasimha, the half-man, half-lion
avatar of Vishnu.

Narasimha killed Hiranyakashipu in a way that did not violate the
boon: - Not inside or outside (on a threshold) - Not day or night (at
twilight) - Not by man or animal (half-man, half-lion) - Not with a
weapon (used claws)

Prahlad’s devotion and faith were rewarded, and he became a symbol of
true devotion and righteousness.

Moral: - True devotion always wins - God protects sincere devotees - Ego
and arrogance lead to destruction
